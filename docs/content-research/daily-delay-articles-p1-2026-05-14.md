# Daily flight-delay articles P1 research - 2026-05-14

Scope: six new bilingual detailed articles under the flight-delay main guide:
`flight-delay-assistance-thresholds`, `overnight-flight-delay-hotel-transfer-proof`,
`airport-closure-flight-delay-compensation`, `deicing-flight-delay-compensation`,
`extra-costs-after-flight-delay`, and `rerouting-another-airline-flight-delay`.

Official-source baseline:
- European Commission passenger-rights guidance: delay assistance is separate from fixed compensation; care can include meals, refreshments, communication, hotel accommodation and transport when needed; long delays can create refund/rerouting questions.
- EU Regulation 261/2004 concepts used for the article structure: waiting-time assistance thresholds, final-destination arrival, rerouting under comparable transport conditions, and extraordinary-circumstances review.

Competitor and market review used for coverage:
- AirHelp: passenger-facing delay pages emphasize final-arrival timing, 250/400/600 EUR bands, meals and refreshments, hotel during overnight disruption, and extraordinary circumstances.
- Flightright: delay guides emphasize three-hour arrival, route coverage, airline responsibility, care during long waits, and evidence after airline refusal.
- Skycop: guidance covers delay compensation, care rights, extraordinary circumstances, and practical evidence for claims.
- AirAdvisor: review used for treatment of long delays, reimbursement, rerouting and airline-defence patterns.
- ClaimCompass: review used for delay basics, documentation, and separation of compensation from care costs.

Topic notes:
- Assistance thresholds article: built around the 2/3/4-hour care thresholds and the difference between care and fixed compensation.
- Overnight hotel article: focused on hotel, transfer and receipt evidence because competitor pages commonly mention care but rarely explain how to package proof.
- Airport closure article: treats closure as a potentially strong extraordinary-circumstances defence while preserving care-cost and post-reopening timeline checks.
- De-icing article: separates normal winter operations from extreme weather or airport restrictions, with evidence around changing airline reasons.
- Extra-costs article: separates care costs, rerouting/new tickets and weaker consequential losses so the public copy does not overpromise recovery.
- Rerouting article: focuses on written offers, final-destination arrival, one-booking connections and caution before self-buying replacement transport.

Public-copy constraints:
