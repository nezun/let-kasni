## Scope

This note covers the six detailed flight-delay articles added under the flight-delay
main guide. They are not standalone main guides; they support the main page by going
deeper on duration, airport actions, evidence, care rights, and final-arrival timing.

## Sources and patterns used

| Source | Useful observed pattern |
| --- | --- |
| AirHelp flight-delay compensation | 3-hour final-arrival rule, compensation amounts, right to care, documents, and service-led claim handling. |
| AirHelp EU delay page | EU-specific eligibility, covered routes, non-eligible cases, filing support, and care/refund separation. |
| Skycop delayed-flight compensation | Thresholds, delay calculation, care rights, time limits, extraordinary circumstances, and FAQ structure. |
| FairPlane flight-delay page | Destination-arrival threshold, care from waiting thresholds, hotel/meal support, retroactive claims, and lawyer/service enforcement framing. |
| EUclaim flight-delay page | 250/400/600 EUR table, connecting flights, required documents, non-claim cases, and common passenger questions. |
| Official Your Europe passenger-rights page | Official compensation amounts, care rights, rerouting/refund rules, final-arrival logic, and complaint sequencing. |

## Approved outline logic

Each article uses H2s that fit one of the P1 reasons: direct search intent, a
legal/eligibility condition, or a competitor/official-source covered user step.
No H2 is about our internal SEO structure, calculator usage, or link architecture.

## Visual/checklist modules

Each article has at least one structured checklist module and at least one process,
step, evidence, table-intent, or care-rights section. Hero images, the mandatory
quick-check CTA and related-guide cards do not count. The quick-check CTA belongs
immediately after the first H2 section body and opens the claim flow directly.
These modules are used as compact in-body visuals for standard articles until
article-specific interactive components are added.
